Facilities

Laboratory for large animal research:

The Anesthesia Center for Critical Research is based in ~6000 square feet of newly-renovated laboratory space on Thier 5 located on the MGH main campus. The ACCCR has approximately 2000 square feet of laboratory space dedicated to his research programs. The laboratory is equipped to meet or exceed all standards for small and large animal studies and for the safe use of radioisotopes, recombinant DNA, and chemical hazards. It has a dedicated area for tissue culture, histology, and fluorescent imaging. There are four large -80°C refrigerators for storage of plasma and tissue samples. A common dark room and cold room are also available in the Thier 5 laboratory.
A state-of-the-art large animal surgical suite is located in the ACCCR laboratory is dedicated to physiological studies of anesthetized and awake animals that can be housed for several days. The room is surrounded by a pre-op room, a post-op room, ventilation and an integrated supply of medical gases and vacuum sources. The operating room also contains built-in stainless steel furniture with an integrated foot-pedal-operated sink, 15 foot long table surface and glassed storage cabinets with over 75 feet of shelves. The lab is also fitted with a ultrasonography room dedicated to in vivo cardiac physiology, which displays a Vivid 7 GE and a Visualsonic VEVO 770 mounting a 30MHz probe to allow for high resolution scans of right heart chambers in mice.
The ACCCR enterprises some of the most challenging transaltional medicine models, with focus on small rodent physiology and cardiovascular responses to breathing various inhaled gas mixtures.
Four large fume hoods are available, of which two are currently employed for special mouse housing and breathing of different gas mixtures including nitric oxide (NO) and nitrogen (N2)
and for the monitoring of nitrogen dioxide (NO2) and carbon dioxide (CO2), (see animal
section). Compressed gases, delivered either by wall sources, cylinders, or nitrogen
generators are continuously monitored by staff and research fellows to allow proper delivery of inhaled therapies and environmental safety at the same time.

Small Rodent Physiology

The ACCCR laboratory has 7 custom-designed stations designed ad hoc for in vivo basic preclinical research with specific focus on murine cardiopulmonary physiology. One of these is outfitted with a Millar Pressure-Volume Catheter System for simultaneous measurement of left ventricular pressures, volumes and cardiac output. All benches are provided with mouse ventilators, first class Hans Rudolph pneumotachographers and Ponemah amplifiers for multiparameter data acquisition.
A system to provide inhaled anesthesia is available on each bench top, with dedicated vacuum sources to remove waste gases. Each station is also equipped with individualized gas outlets including air, oxygen and other gases such as NO, N2, CO2 and CO which are supplied via cylinders, allowing to obtain desired gas mixtures. Waste removal is provided with special vacuum systems on each bench top.
High precision flowmeters (Cole Parmer) are used to maximise the precision of gas mixtures. High precision
oxygen analyzers (Sable systems) and carbon dioxide analysers (Li-820) are also available for respirometry and metabolic studies of small rodents.

The ACCCR lab owns a special permit in compliance with IACUC and AAALAC regulations which approves extraordinary housing of mice directly in the lab. Animals living in standard cages, provided with unlimited access to food and water are housed inside acrylic chambers, which are placed inside the fume hoods to allow proper waste gas removal and can easily be accessed by members of the laboratory. Transparent chambers allow for direct supervision operated directly by investigators, along with routine monitoring of animal health and veterinarian supervision.
Nine different chambers are available to study the separate therapeutic effects of inhaling various gas mixtures, ranging from 20 to 200 liters in size. Overall, this satellite facility is capable of housing more than 120 mice at one time for chronic experiments that can last up to several months. Adequate chamber ventilation is guaranteed with a continuous flow of different gas mixtures.
